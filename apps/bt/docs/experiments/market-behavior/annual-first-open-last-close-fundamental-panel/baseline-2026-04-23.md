## Interpretation

1. The central signal is value, not broad quality. Low `PBR`, low
   `forward PER`, and low `PER` had larger and cleaner spreads than ROE,
   equity ratio, CFO margin, raw EPS/BPS levels, or `forward EPS / actual EPS`.
2. `standard` is the most attractive broad market surface in this run:
   good return, better Sharpe, and less extreme drawdown than `growth`.
3. `growth` low-PBR and small-cap branches produced high returns but much
   deeper drawdowns and smaller name counts. Treat them as separate high-risk
   experiments.
4. Small cap and low ADV materially improve historical returns, but they are
   likely to absorb a large hidden cost in live execution. Before production,
   test minimum ADV floors, position-size caps, and slippage assumptions.
5. The per-share adjustment is not optional. About 18% of realized events had
   adjusted EPS/BPS/forward EPS due to entry-date share baseline differences.

## Research Close

This research is closed at the descriptive/baseline stage. The next research
branch, if needed, should be a tradeable selection study with:

- `standard` as the primary market.
- Low `PBR` and low `forward PER` as the core value filters.
- `forward EPS / actual EPS` only as a diagnostic or tie-break candidate,
  not as a core filter from this run.
- Dividend yield or CFO yield as secondary ranking/tie-break features.
- Explicit ADV floor, market-cap floor, position count, and slippage/capacity
  assumptions.
- Out-of-sample or walk-forward validation instead of selecting thresholds
  from the full `2017-2025` period.

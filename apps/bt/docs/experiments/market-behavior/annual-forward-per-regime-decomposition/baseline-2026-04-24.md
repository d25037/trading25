## Main Conclusion

The earlier `low forward PER` signal is not just one thing.

In `all` and `standard`, the stronger piece is **positive low forward PER**:
cheap stocks with still-positive forecast EPS. That regime has the highest
event-level return and the strongest independent regression coefficient.

But **non-positive forward PER** also contributes positively. It is not the main
driver, yet it is not noise either. The current full `low forward PER` factor
worked because it mixed:

1. a stronger positive low-forward-PER value effect
2. a weaker but still positive non-positive-forward-PER turnaround/distress effect

## Practical Read

The best practical interpretation is:

1. Do not treat `low forward PER` as a single economic story.
2. The cleaner alpha source is `positive low forward PER`.
3. `non-positive forward PER` is a real but rougher secondary sleeve.
4. For `standard`, the best next score family is not "ban non-positive" and not
   "all-in non-positive". It is closer to a decomposed mix where `positive low`
   gets the larger weight and `non-positive` is allowed but smaller.

So the next iteration should move from a single low-forward-PER factor toward a
two-part implementation:

- `positive low forward PER` as the primary valuation input
- `non-positive forward PER` as a separate optional turnaround overlay

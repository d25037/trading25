## Main Conclusion

The prior result is not just one variable wearing another variable's clothes.
Low `PBR`, small market cap, and low `forward PER` each keep meaningful
independent signal after controlling for the others plus year / market / sector
fixed effects.

The weaker result is `low ADV60`: once market cap is in the model, low ADV no
longer looks like a clean standalone effect. It is better treated as a capacity
and execution-risk axis rather than a primary alpha factor.

VIF confirms this:

- Dividend yield and forecast dividend yield have VIF around `8-10`.
- Prime small cap / low ADV / valuation variables have elevated but manageable
  VIF around `2.5-4.3`.
- The value and size factors are correlated, but not enough to erase their
  independent coefficients.

## Practical Read

The best next tradeable branch is not `low PBR + low ADV`. It is closer to:

1. `standard`
2. low `PBR`
3. small market cap, but with explicit minimum liquidity and capacity controls
4. low `forward PER` as the next additive value filter
5. yield factors only as secondary tie-breaks
6. `forward EPS / actual EPS` as diagnostic only

Before production use, this still needs explicit cost/slippage/capacity tests
and walk-forward threshold selection.

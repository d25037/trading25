## Main Conclusion

This practical rerun does not overturn the earlier read.

Once obviously hard-to-buy names with non-positive `PBR` or non-positive
`forward PER` are removed, low `PBR`, small market cap, and low `forward PER`
still remain the durable core in `all`, `prime`, and `standard`.

What did change is `growth`: low `forward PER` stops looking useful there after
the positive-ratio filter. That means the earlier `growth` signal was likely
helped by distressed names with negative denominators.

## Practical Read

For a more realistic research branch, it is reasonable to enforce
`PBR > 0` and `forward PER > 0` by default when the downstream use case is
actual screening.

The cost is a smaller universe. The benefit is a cleaner interpretation:

1. `standard`
2. low `PBR`
3. small market cap
4. low `forward PER`
5. treat `ADV60` as execution/capacity control, not as a primary factor

That means the next score-based branch can safely keep `low forward PER`
without depending on negative-ratio edge cases.

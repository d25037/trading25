## Interpretation

1. Growth weakness is not just a high-PBR story. High PBR is visible, but the
   larger divergence is a cluster of non-positive EPS/forward-PER regimes,
   forecast missing, weak CFO diagnostics, weak/absent dividend metrics, and
   weaker ROA.
2. Standard is not simply "bad small-cap". It is much smaller and less liquid
   than Prime, but it is value-cheap and does not have Growth's severe
   non-positive/fallback diagnostics.
3. The return gap decomposition says "avoid Growth" remains a meaningful
   market-level prior even after the current fundamental controls. If trying to
   rescue Growth, it should be a separate quality-screened Growth study, not a
   broad inclusion in the same value universe.

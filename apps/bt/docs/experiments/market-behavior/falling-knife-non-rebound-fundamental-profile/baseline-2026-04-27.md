## Interpretation

The better framing is descriptive:

> non-rebound names are enriched for Growth, negative profit, low quality score, high Daily RAR bucket, and negative forecast EPS.

However, the fundamental explanation is incomplete. Growth high-quality names still have a high non-rebound rate (`52.85%`) and high severe-loss rate (`19.44%`). This suggests that Growth market microstructure / sentiment regime / valuation unwind is still important even after coarse fundamental quality is controlled.

Fundamental features that look useful:

- `Profit <= 0`: broad and clean; strongest single fundamental feature by prevalence lift.
- `quality_score < 3`: broader but weaker per-trade lift.
- `forecast EPS <= 0`: smaller sample but higher non-rebound rate.
- `PBR >= 3x`: strongest valuation-side bad feature after correcting BPS to latest FY.
- `forward PER >= 40x`: bad feature, but weaker than high PBR after FY correction.
- `forward PER < 15x` and `PBR < 1x`: protective value-like features; both are under-represented in non-rebound events.
- `CFO <= 0`, `FCF <= 0`, and `equity ratio < 30%`: weak as standalone non-rebound discriminators in this first pass.

Protective segments are Prime and higher quality scores, especially `quality_score=5`, positive CFO/FCF, positive profit, and high-quality buckets. These reduce non-rebound probability but do not fully explain the Growth effect.
